B · Dusk / Icon-First Dark — native to the icon's world

C · Twilight Gradient — warm sunset into violet night

The window itself is a vertical gradient: cream at the title bar, through peach and mauve, settling into the icon's deep violet at the foot. D · Parchment & Glow — setup gate, light surface + icon halo

Applies the hybrid specifically to the first-run setup screen. Today's frosted white card on a pastel gradient stays, but a miniature of the new app icon sits at the top, and the whole card rests on a subtle violet halo that radiates up from behind — the same glow the icon produces. Check marks and the primary CTA adopt the icon's deep-violet palette to unify the brand.

How to pick: if the app's warmth is a core feeling you want to protect, go with A. If you want the product to feel premium and cinematic and match the icon's mood, B. C is a statement piece — great for marketing, bold in daily use. D shows how any hybrid could be rolled out gradually, starting with the setup screen where the icon is most visible. All four pull from the same token set: ink #14102a, violet #8b5cff, amber #deb87a, cream #f4ebd4.
